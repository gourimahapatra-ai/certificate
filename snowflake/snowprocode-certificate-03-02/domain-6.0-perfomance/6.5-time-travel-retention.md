# ⏳ Snowflake Time Travel & Retention Best Practices

Time Travel is useful for:
- 🔄 Data recovery  
- 🕒 Historical access  

However:
- 💰 It **increases storage cost**
- 📦 Also impacts **Fail-safe storage**

> 🎯 Goal: Balance **data protection vs cost optimization**

---

## 🧠 1. Key Concept

- Time Travel retains **historical data versions**
- Fail-safe (7 days) stores additional backup (non-configurable)

---

## 🏗️ 2. Staging Tables / Databases

### ✅ Best Practice
- Set retention = **0 days**
- Use **Transient Tables**

---

### 💡 Why?
- Data is:
  - Temporary
  - Easily reloadable from source
  - Not business-critical

---

### 🎯 Benefits
- ❌ No Time Travel storage
- ❌ No Fail-safe cost
- 💰 Maximum cost savings

---

## 🏭 3. Production Tables

### ✅ Best Practice
- Set retention = **1–7 days**

---

### 💡 Why?
- Data is:
  - Business-critical
  - Needs recovery capability

---

### ⚠️ Recommendation
- Minimum: **1 day**
- Avoid unnecessarily high retention

---

## 🔄 4. High-Churn / Large Tables (Special Case)

### 🚨 Problem Scenario
- Example:
  - Table size = **20 GB**
  - Updated **20 times/day**

---

### 💸 Cost Impact
- Time Travel (1 day):
  - 20 GB × 20 updates = **400 GB**
- Fail-safe (7 days):
  - 400 GB × 7 = **2.8 TB**

---

### ⚠️ Insight
> Storage cost grows **exponentially** with frequent updates

---

### ✅ Optimization Strategy
- Use **Transient Tables**
- Set retention = **0 days** (if acceptable)

---

### 🎯 When to Apply
- High update frequency
- Large tables
- Non-critical or reproducible data

---

## ⚖️ 5. Decision Guidelines

| Use Case | Table Type | Retention |
|----------|-----------|----------|
| Staging | Transient | 0 days |
| Development | Transient / Temporary | 0–1 day |
| Production | Permanent | 1–7 days |
| High-churn large tables | Transient (if possible) | 0 days |

---

## 🚀 Key Takeaways

- ⏳ Time Travel increases **storage cost**
- 📦 Fail-safe adds **additional 7-day cost**
- 🎯 Use:
  - **0 days** → staging / transient workloads
  - **1–7 days** → production data
- ⚠️ Be careful with:
  - Large + frequently updated tables

---

> 💡 Optimize retention based on **data criticality + update frequency**
